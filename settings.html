How this works?

Data Storage in the Line Chart

The system stores data at specific intervals to ensure consistent and efficient chart updates. Each predefined interval is divided into 12 equal parts, allowing for frequent data capture and smooth chart visualization.

Interval Breakdown:

  • 10 minutes divided by 12 = 0.83 minutes (approximately every 50 seconds)
  • 15 minutes divided by 12 = 1.25 minutes (every 1 minute and 15 seconds)
  • 30 minutes divided by 12 = 2.5 minutes (every 2 minutes and 30 seconds)
  • 1 hour divided by 12 = 5 minutes
  • 2 hours divided by 12 = 10 minutes

Purpose of This Approach:

  • Consistent Data Sampling: Dividing each interval into 12 ensures even data collection throughout any selected time range.
  • Optimized Performance: Data is stored frequently enough to capture trends without overloading the system with excessive points.
  • Flexible Visualization: Users can choose different intervals to control the granularity of the data displayed, providing a clear balance between detail and readability.

This system ensures that data is stored and displayed efficiently, making it easier to track trends and insights over time without compromising performance.
